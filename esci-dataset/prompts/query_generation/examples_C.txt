## Examples - Complement (C)

These queries would cause the anchor to appear as a complement/pairing in search results.
**IMPORTANT**: The anchor is the search result that appears as a pairing. Generate queries that users would search for which this anchor would appear as a complement/pairing suggestion.

**DO NOT include or imply the anchor item in the query.** Queries should ONLY mention the complement item—the anchor appears as a pairing suggestion. The anchor should not be remotely implied.

**What NOT to do:**
- **Anchor: Ice Cream** ❌ "brownie + ice cream" (mentions anchor) ❌ "brownie and something cold" (implies anchor) ✅ "brownie"
- **Anchor: French Fries** ❌ "burger and fries" (mentions anchor) ❌ "burger with sides" (implies anchor) ✅ "burger"
- **Anchor: Garlic Bread** ❌ "pizza with garlic bread" (mentions anchor) ❌ "pizza and bread" (implies anchor) ✅ "pizza"
- **Anchor: Croissant** ❌ "coffee and croissant" (mentions anchor) ❌ "coffee and something savory" (implies anchor) ✅ "coffee"
- **Anchor: Cookies** ❌ "chai and cookies" (mentions anchor) ❌ "chai snacks" (implies anchor/pairing) ✅ "chai" or "evening chai"

**Complement patterns:**
1. Functional pairings (condiment → main): Raita→"biryani", Naan→"curry"
2. Substantial accompaniments (side → main): French Fries→"burger", Garlic Bread→"pizza"
3. Course pairings (appetizer ↔ main ↔ dessert): Tomato Soup→"pasta", Chicken Wings→"pizza"
4. Multi-item same-category (main ↔ main, app ↔ app): Pizza↔"pasta", Samosa↔"pakora"
5. Beverage pairings: Coffee→"dessert", Masala Chai→"snacks", Milkshake→"burger"
6. Regional bundles: thali components, combo meals, breakfast platters
